I’m Nicolás Bronzina, an Argentinean Design Researcher living in Madrid

The website is programmed using HTML code from a text editor on my Mac computer, based on static sites.

As a Social Scientist and Researcher with a Master's in User Experience Design, I am deeply committed to advancing the speculative aspects of design. My Design Researcher role allows me to enrich my work through extensive research, diegetic prototyping, and translating conceptual frameworks into tangible ideas. My focus areas include the future of food, urban environments, ecology, and emerging technologies.

In addition to being a managing partner at BPP, I contribute to design fiction writing and collaborate with Girardin & Nova, where I conduct in-depth research on emerging trends and 'weak signals' to identify potential future scenarios. Recently, I have also started designing diegetic artifacts there. I am actively involved with Heated Consultancy, where I leverage my expertise to explore innovative solutions for climate resilience, striving to positively impact an ever-changing world.

Projects

San Telmo Market

A Design Fiction work that explores the City of Buenos Aires's potential future and gastronomy through the evolution of one of its iconic markets, the San Telmo Market. It shows the market's different stalls, shops, and services influenced by long periods of extreme heat and droughts. A work led by Nicolás Bronzina in collaboration with Fabien Girardin and Israel Viadest at Girardin & Nova. Futures in Maps is an atlas that tells stories about a world we all might experience soon.

OFFicial: Welcome

This website and its accompanying artifact narrate a plausible future related to the refugee experience we may all encounter soon, irrespective of our location in Spain. It forms a part of the Final Master’s Thesis based on Futures Design and Strategic Foresight, a collaborative effort by Nicolás Bronzina, Eider Fernández Árcos, and Jorge Gil, titled "User Experience Design as a Tool for Social Intervention in the Refugee Crisis." Employing an approach known as Design Fiction, we aimed to envision, create, and advance preferable futures, actively resisting the passive acceptance of the current and impending challenges associated with the refugee crisis.

Heated DANA's NatGeo Special Edition

A design fiction capsule that imagines a mural of resilience on the facades of Andalusia, created by artists and local communities to represent strength and solidarity in the face of the DANA floods 2024. This diegetic artifact uses public art as a visual and emotional reminder of the need for more robust environmental policies and community capacity to address climate change. Part of "The Sociological Significance of Design Fiction Artefacts: Navigating Collective Trauma and Building Community Resilience After a Climate Disaster".
