WELCOME to OFFicial

A speculative design fiction project envisioning a comprehensive support system for displaced communities in 2030.

This project explores how technology and UX design can empower refugees through education, employment, and community integration.

About This Project

OFFicial is a design fiction project created as part of a Master's thesis at UDIT Madrid. It envisions a future where displaced communities have access to comprehensive digital tools for education, employment, and social integration.

The Story Behind OFFicial

Why Design Fiction?

Design fiction allows us to explore possible futures and their implications without the constraints of current technology or policy. By creating a detailed, believable world set in 2030, we can:

  • Provoke meaningful discussion about refugee integration
  • Prototype complex socio-technical systems
  • Imagine preferred futures that inspire action today
  • Test assumptions about technology's role in social challenges

The Research Question

How can UX design serve as a vehicle for social intervention, specifically in supporting displaced communities?

Project Goals

  • Demonstrate empathy-driven design at scale
  • Explore systemic approaches to complex social problems
  • Create tangible artifacts that make abstract futures concrete
  • Contribute to the discourse on technology ethics and social responsibility

Academic Framework

This project combines:

  • Speculative Design - Creating provocative what-if scenarios
  • Critical Design - Questioning assumptions about technology
  • Service Design - Mapping complex service ecosystems
  • User Experience Design - Prioritizing human needs

OFFicial Services

In our 2030 scenario, OFFicial provides a comprehensive ecosystem of support services:

1. WELCOME APP

Mobile-first personal assistant for newly arrived refugees featuring:

5. WELCOME Home

Design Principles

The WELCOME system is built on core design principles:

  • Dignity First - Every interaction respects user agency
  • Accessibility - Multilingual, multi-modal, inclusive
  • Trust & Transparency - Clear data usage, no surprises
  • Cultural Sensitivity - Adaptive to diverse backgrounds
  • Empowerment - Tools that increase autonomy

Visual Language

Our interface uses:

Interaction Design

  • Offline-First - Core functions work without connectivity

Research & Methodology

This project employed multiple research methodologies to create a rigorous, credible design fiction:

Ethnographic Research

Interviews with refugees, social workers, and integration specialists to understand real needs, challenges, and aspirations.

Stakeholder Analysis

Mapping the complex ecosystem of government agencies, NGOs, employers, and community organizations.

Speculative Design

Creating a believable 2030 scenario grounded in current trends in AI, mobile technology, and policy development.

Service Design

Mapping user journeys, touchpoints, and service blueprints for the entire WELCOME ecosystem.

Diegetic Prototyping

Creating artifacts that exist within the fictional world - apps, websites, promotional materials - to make the speculation tangible.

UX Design

Applying user-centered design principles to create interfaces and experiences that prioritize dignity and empowerment.

Design Fiction as Research Tool

Design fiction serves multiple research purposes:

  • Exploration - Testing assumptions about future scenarios
  • Communication - Making abstract concepts concrete and discussable
  • Provocation - Challenging current approaches and assumptions
  • Inspiration - Offering aspirational visions that motivate action

Ethical Considerations

This project carefully considered:

  • Data privacy and security for vulnerable populations
  • Avoiding surveillance or control mechanisms
  • Cultural sensitivity and avoiding stereotypes
  • Empowerment vs. dependency
  • Technology optimism vs. critical perspective
